Syllable structure processes

Stopping A fricative or affricate consonant is replaced by a stop consonant. zoo → duː; chip → tɪp
Fronting Back consonants are replaced with ones articulated further forward. shoe → suː; goat → dəʊt
Gliding Liquids /l/ or /r/ are replaced by glides /w/ or /j/. leg → jeg; rabbit → wæbɪt
Vocalization A vowel replaces a syllabic consonant. apple → æpo; bottom → bɒtu
Consonant voicing Voiceless consonants become voiced. pet → bet; tiny → daɪni
Consonant devoicing Final consonants become voiceless. pig → pɪk; toes → dəʊs
Consonant harmony A consonant assimilates to another in the word. duck → gʌk; nipple → mɪpu
Consonant cluster reduction Clusters are reduced to one consonant. snow → nəʊ
Vowel epenthesis A vowel is added to break up a cluster. blue → bəlu
Deletion of final consonants Final consonant is omitted. bike → baɪ
Syllable deletion An unstressed syllable is deleted. banana → nɑːnə
Reduplication A CV syllable is repeated. water → wɔːwɔː
