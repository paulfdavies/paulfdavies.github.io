What are the sentence types?

Simple: one independent clause (one subject + one finite verb). No dependent clauses.

Compound: two or more independent clauses joined by a coordinating conjunction (for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so) or a semicolon.

Complex: one independent clause with at least one dependent (subordinate) clause (e.g., starting with because, although, when, if, who/which/that).

Minor: an incomplete clause used communicatively (e.g., headlines, interjections, directives with ellipsis): Silence, please. / No phones. / Thanks!
